Don't Put Your Best Thinking Everywhere. Put It Exactly Where It Matters.

By Pratik Dhanave
Multi-ModelAI AgentsCostClaudeArchitecture

Most teams using AI coding agents face the same quiet tradeoff: use a powerful model for everything (great results, painful costs), or a cheaper model for everything (great costs, weaker judgment on the hard calls). Claude's advisor capability quietly removes that tradeoff.

The idea: a fast, low-cost model does the bulk of the work. But the moment it hits something genuinely hard — an architecture decision, an ambiguous bug, a plan that isn't converging — it taps a smarter model on the shoulder, asks for guidance, and gets right back to work. You pay premium rates only for the handful of moments that actually shape the outcome. Everything else runs fast and cheap.

What I find interesting isn't the cost savings. It's the design philosophy:

Don't put your best thinking everywhere. Put it exactly where it matters.

That's a principle that goes well beyond code. It's how good teams already operate — junior talent moves the work forward, senior expertise steps in at the decision points. The advisor just makes AI work the same way. The open question for anyone running agents in production: are you routing by task complexity yet?
